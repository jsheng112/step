First Day at Google

Today is my first day as a Google STEP intern! I am so excited to start this internship and even though the internship format was changed, I still hope to learn many things this summer. This internship structure is very interesting because in the semester that just ended I had just taken a course in web development. Through this internship, I hope to learn more about other languages/techniques/frameworks commonly used in web applications. For me, this summer is my rite of passage into software engineering professionalism. I hope that our capstone final project will be one that makes a difference in the world.

December 7, 2019

IgniteSTEM xPrinceton Conference

Today is the day of the IgniteSTEM xPrinceton Conference. I got up at 5:30am in the morning in eager anticipation of the conference, and also because there were many things to setup before it all starts. A little about IgniteSTEM: IgniteSTEM is one of the many organizations under the Entrepreneurship club at Princeton University. It is a student-run, nonprofit organization with the goal of empowering K-12 educators with the skills, resources, and networks they need to implement project based learning in public schools. I am on the content team and have been involved with IgniteSTEM since freshman year of college. There are usually three or four conferences that we organize every year, and xPrinceton is the first conference of the school year. As a STEM student, I really felt that my high school teachers had a profound influence on my choice of major and career path. If not for them, I am not so sure that I would be where I am today, studying what I am studying today. I want to give back to the education community through IgniteSTEM, and I am proud to be an IgniteSTEM member.

June 23th, 2019

First Day at Tezign

Hi everyone! I’m Jenny, a freshman computer science major, and this is my first blog post! This summer I will be interning with Tezign as a tech intern in product management and R&D, specifically in the AI Marketing PM team. I feel that after a week of work at Tezign it’s about the right time for me to write this journal entry, because things have sort of started to get into a routine, and it's a good place for me to stop and reflect on my experiences so far. I’ve been interested in entrepreneurship and startups for a long time, so I thought that the Princeton Startup Immersion programming would be a great way to experience the startup atmosphere. Besides learning technical skills related to software engineering, I really hope to learn more about what being a software engineer in a startup meant, and some of the product management aspects of a startup. As a cs major there are many different career paths to take, so I see this internship as an opportunity to experience the breadth of paths in startup companies as well as learn more technical skills.

There are many other interns at Tezign as well, and throughout the week I was kind of impressed at the rate that the company is expanding. Almost every other day some new intern or employee would be added to the group chat. I also attended one of the product management meetings, and it was my first company meeting at Tezign. During the meeting I was surprised that although the manager who was leading the meeting spoke regularly, she did not mind at all when some other coworker cut her off to express their ideas. Everyone was freely discussing and sharing their ideas.

I arrived a week before they were about to move location. Tezign is at max capacity and about to move to a newer, bigger location. But even though this would mean that I would have a longer commute, I look forward to spending the rest of my summer at Tezign.
